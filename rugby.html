brief rugby knowlege

Brute strength, speed, agility and tactical thinking are all elements associated with this full-body, contact sport played by nations around the world.
Rugby, which is a variation on football, has been around since the 19th century.
The 15-side team attempts to breach the opposing team’s defensive line and score a try in their in-goal area. The game is played with ball-in-hand and by kicking the ball.
Passing between players is permitted provided it is thrown sideways or backwards.
A rugby game lasts 80 minutes, which is split into two halves of 40 minutes.
A rugby team is made up of 15 players in some of the following positions: loose-head prop, hooker, tight-head prop, second-row lock, flanker, scrumhalf, flyhalf, left-wing, inside centre, outside centre, right-wing and fullback.

Learn some of the most common rugby lingo to improve your betting chances:

Scrum - is a way of restarting play where the players pack closely together and attempt to gain possession of the rugby ball.

Line-out - is a way of restarting play after the ball has gone to touch (off the field).

Offsides - this is a line where any player who crosses before the set-piece is completed, risks a penalty

Drop goal - a kick at the posts which, if successful, earns the team three points

Knock on - is when a player loses, drops or knocks the ball forward from a player’s hand. The ball is then awarded to the opposing team.

Try - is the most valuable play to score. This is achieved by touching the ball down on the opposing team’s in-goal area. This action awards the team five points and the right to earn a conversion kick.

Conversion kick - is awarded to the team who achieved a try and earns an additional two points.
